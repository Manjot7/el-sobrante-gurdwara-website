Khalsa School

Punjabi language, Gurmukhi script, and Sikh heritage for the next generation.

Our Mission

Keeping the next generation
connected to their roots

The Sikh Center of San Francisco Bay Area has offered educational programs for children of the sangat since the Gurdwara's early years. The goal has always been the same: ensure that young Sikhs growing up in the Bay Area remain connected to their language, scripture, and heritage.

Classes meet on Sunday mornings alongside the diwan, so families need only a single visit each week. Children learn Gurmukhi script, Punjabi reading and conversation, Sikh history, and the significance of our banis and ceremonies — giving them the foundation to participate fully in their faith community as they grow.

In addition to weekly Sunday classes, the Gurdwara runs summer camps for students from kindergarten through 12th grade, covering Sikh history, kirtan and music, and general academics — with meals provided. Teachers are volunteer members of the sangat. No prior Punjabi knowledge is required to enroll.

What We Teach

🔤

Gurmukhi Script

Learning to read and write the Gurmukhi alphabet — the script of Sri Guru Granth Sahib Ji. Students progress from letter recognition to reading complete banis.

🗣️

Punjabi Language

Conversational Punjabi so students can communicate with grandparents, family, and the broader Punjabi community. Vocabulary, grammar, and everyday phrases.

📜

Sikh History & Heritage

Stories of the ten Gurus, the significance of Sikh symbols and ceremonies, and the history of the Khalsa — told in age-appropriate ways for each level.

🙏

Nitnem & Bani

Learning the daily prayers — Japji Sahib, Rehras Sahib, Ardas — with pronunciation, meaning, and the context of when each bani is recited.

🎵

Kirtan Basics

Introduction to kirtan for interested students — clapping rhythms, simple shabads, and the tradition of devotional music in Sikhi.

🌍

Sikh Values & Sewa

Discussions around Sikh values — equality, honesty, sewa (selfless service), and how those principles apply to daily life as a young person.

Registration

How to Enroll

  1. Bring your child to any Sunday Khalsa School session to meet the teachers and observe a class.
  2. Complete the paper registration form available at the Gurdwara front desk.
  3. Pay the annual registration fee (covers printed materials for the year).
  4. Classes begin the following Sunday after registration is complete.

Fees

Annual registration: $40 per child
(covers printed learning materials for the year)

Fee waivers available — speak to the Khalsa School coordinator. No child is turned away for inability to pay.
