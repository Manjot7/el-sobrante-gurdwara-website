About Us

Serving the Sikh community
of the Bay Area since 1969

The Sikh Center of San Francisco Bay Area — home of Gurdwara Sahib El Sobrante — has been serving the Bay Area Sikh community since its establishment in 1969. What began as informal gatherings in Berkeley grew into a formal organization with one purpose: to give the Bay Area's growing Sikh population a dedicated place of worship.

In 1976, approximately five acres of hillside in El Sobrante were purchased for the Gurdwara. The first building phase was completed in May 1979, and the current structure — with its distinctive golden domes and sweeping views of the El Sobrante valley and San Pablo Bay — was completed in June 1992. It stands as one of the most magnificent Gurdwaras in North America.

Rooted in the teachings of Sri Guru Granth Sahib Ji, the Gurdwara is open to all regardless of faith or background. Langar — a free community meal — is served daily to every visitor. Regular diwan is held every Sunday morning and Wednesday evening, and the Gurdwara runs educational programs for the children of the sangat throughout the year.
